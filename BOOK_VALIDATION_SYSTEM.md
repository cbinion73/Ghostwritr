# GHOSTWRITR BOOK VALIDATION SYSTEM
## Market Viability as the Gating Mechanism for All Books

**Effective Date:** April 2026  
**Applies To:** Every book concept in GHOSTWRITR  
**Owner:** GHOSTWRITR Platform  
**Purpose:** Prevent weak concepts from reaching writing stage; ensure every book published has evidence-based market viability

---

## CORE PRINCIPLE

**No book advances past Promise Stage without achieving 3.5+ viability score.**

This is not optional. This is the fundamental gate that determines whether a book concept:
- Gets outlined
- Gets written
- Gets published
- Gets marketed

## PROMISE STAGE: THE VALIDATION GATE

### What Happens in Promise Stage

**Duration:** 6 weeks minimum  
**Input:** Book concept (problem + promise + target audience)  
**Output:** Market viability artifact + go/no-go decision

### Week 1-2: Problem Validation
**Questions Answered:**
- Is this a real problem? (frequency, severity, evidence)
- Is the target audience actively trying to solve it? (market spend, competitive solutions)
- How painful is it? (time, cost, career impact - quantified)

**Gate Check:** If problem score <3.5, pause and refine problem statement.

### Week 2-3: Audience & Competitive Landscape
**Questions Answered:**
- Who will actually buy this? (specific persona with buying power)
- How will we reach them? (3+ realistic distribution channels)
- What are competitors promising? (5-10 comparable solutions mapped)
- Where are the market gaps? (opportunities for differentiation)

**Gate Check:** If audience accessibility <3.0 or competition score <3.5, document strategy before proceeding.

### Week 3-4: Differentiation & Practical Value
**Questions Answered:**
- How is this book genuinely different? (can it be explained in one sentence?)
- Will readers actually USE it? (concrete tools, templates, frameworks)
- What will readers DO differently? (specific actions, timeline, outcomes)

**Gate Check:** If differentiation <2.5 or usability <2.5, rewrite promise statement.

### Week 4-5: Completion & Shareability & Commercial Viability
**Questions Answered:**
- Will busy professionals finish reading it? (structure, pacing, early wins)
- Will readers recommend it? (memorable concepts, viral potential)
- Will it sell enough to justify writing? (revenue projection, ROI)
- Is the author credible? (experience, authority, audience trust)

**Gate Check:** If completion <3.0 or commercial potential unclear, document content/structure changes.

### Week 5-6: Synthesis & Final Verdict
**Output:**
- Weighted viability score (1-5)
- 11-dimension scorecard
- Go/no-go decision
- If refining: specific action plan with timeline

### THE GATE DECISION

```
Final Viability Score: __/5.0

Score 4.0-5.0 → ✓ PROCEED TO OUTLINE
               Confidence: High
               Action: Move to Stage 2 immediately

Score 3.5-3.99 → ⚠ CONDITIONAL PROCEED
                Conditions: Specific improvements must be made
                Timeline: 2-4 weeks to refine
                Action: Refine, re-validate, then move to Stage 2

Score <3.5 → ✗ DO NOT WRITE
            Action: Archive concept
            Option: Revisit in 6-12 months with major rework
            Rationale: High risk of book failing to sell/finish/apply
```

## WHAT GETS VALIDATED (The 11 Dimensions)

Every book is scored on these 11 dimensions (1-5 scale):

| # | Dimension | What It Tests | Pass Threshold |
|---|-----------|---------------|-----------------|
| 1 | Problem Validation | Is the problem real, frequent, severe, actively being solved? | 3.5+ |
| 2 | Audience Clarity | Can you clearly identify who will buy this? | 3.5+ |
| 3 | Audience Accessibility | Can you actually reach this audience? | 3.0+ |
| 4 | Competitive Landscape | Do competitors exist? Is market gap clear? | 3.5+ |
| 5 | Differentiation | Is your book genuinely different from competitors? | 3.0+ |
| 6 | Practical Usability | Will readers actually apply what they learn? | 2.5+ |
| 7 | Completion Likelihood | Will busy readers finish the book? | 2.5+ |
| 8 | Shareability | Will readers recommend it to peers? | 2.0+ |
| 9 | Timing & Trends | Is this topic growing/stable/declining? Relevant in 3-5 years? | 3.5+ |
| 10 | Commercial Value | Will this sell enough to justify writing? | 2.5+ |
| 11 | Author Credibility | Will readers trust this author? | 2.5+ |

**Weighted Average = Final Viability Score**

## HOW VALIDATION PREVENTS FAILURE

### Before (Without Validation)
- Author writes book based on intuition
- Sends to market unprepared
- Discovers too late: wrong audience, no distribution, boring structure
- Book fails: low sales, high abandonment, no recommendations

**Example:** Generic leadership book #47, similar to 100 others, no clear differentiation. Gets lost on Amazon.

### After (With Validation)
- Author validates problem + audience first
- Discovers competitive landscape + gaps
- Identifies specific differentiation before writing chapter 1
- Designs structure to maximize completion + application
- Develops distribution strategy during writing
- Book succeeds: clear positioning, targeted audience, high engagement

**Example:** "LabFlow Leadership System" - if refined to 4.0+ viability, would have: defined frameworks, workbook structure, university distribution, credible author endorsement, clear differentiation from Heemstra.

## WHO DOES VALIDATION?

### Option 1: Author Self-Validates
- Author completes 13-phase checklist
- Gathers research evidence
- Calculates own viability score
- Honest assessment required

**Pros:** Builds author's market understanding  
**Cons:** Confirmation bias (authors love their own ideas)

### Option 2: GHOSTWRITR Validator (Recommended)
- GHOSTWRITR team member validates with author
- Objective perspective
- Access to GHOSTWRITR research resources
- Expert scoring

**Pros:** Unbiased assessment, higher quality evidence  
**Cons:** Requires GHOSTWRITR team capacity

### Option 3: Peer Validation
- Fellow author reviews validation
- External perspective
- Identifies blind spots

**Pros:** Peer credibility, catches assumptions  
**Cons:** Peer may be biased toward author

**Recommendation:** Combination approach
- Author completes checklist
- GHOSTWRITR validator reviews
- Peer reviews for final score

## INTEGRATION WITH OTHER STAGES

### How Validation Flows to Outline Stage

**Input to Outline:**
```
From PROMISE STAGE (validation):
1. Validated audience persona
2. Competitive differentiation
3. Validated problem/promise
4. Distribution strategy
5. Viability scorecard
6. Any red flags identified
```

**Used in OUTLINE STAGE to:**
- Structure chapters around validated pain points
- Ensure tools/frameworks match validated usability needs
- Design for validated completion likelihood (workbook vs. narrative)
- Plan distribution channels identified in validation
- Address red flags with additional resources/chapters

### How Validation Flows to Research Stage

**Input to Research:**
```
From PROMISE STAGE (validation):
1. Problem severity (quantified)
2. Competitive claims (need to refute/compare)
3. Audience pain points (need to validate)
4. Promised outcomes (need evidence)
```

**Used in RESEARCH STAGE to:**
- Gather evidence supporting problem claims
- Find data to differentiate from competitors
- Validate promises with real-world examples
- Build case studies proving value

## RED FLAGS THAT STOP PROGRESSION

If validation reveals ANY of these, book does not proceed to outline:

### Critical Red Flags (Score <3.5 in ANY dimension)
1. **Problem doesn't exist or isn't severe** (Validation score <2.0)
   - Market not actually paying to solve this
   - Nobody is actively seeking solutions
   - **Stop:** Rework fundamentally or archive

2. **No clear differentiation** (Differentiation score <2.0)
   - Identical to 10 other books
   - Competitor already dominates market
   - **Stop:** Cannot compete, do not write

3. **Author lacks credibility** (Credibility score <1.5)
   - No relevant experience
   - Readers won't trust advice
   - **Stop:** Build credentials first (speaking, research, partnerships)

4. **Cannot reach the audience** (Accessibility score <2.0)
   - No viable distribution channels
   - Audience doesn't read books
   - **Stop:** Find different approach (course, consulting, etc.)

### Warning Flags (Score 2.5-3.5)
- Completion likelihood low → Redesign as workbook
- Practical usability unclear → Add templates/tools
- Commercial viability questionable → Document business case
- Timing off → Explain why proceeding anyway
- Shareability weak → Create memorable framework name

## WHEN VALIDATION SAYS NO

**If final score <3.5, book goes to ARCHIVE stage.**

Archive includes:
- Full validation report (what didn't work)
- Author feedback (why the concept is weak)
- Improvement suggestions (what would need to change)
- Reopen date (when to revisit, if ever)

### Reopen Criteria

Book can be revisited if:
1. Market conditions change (trend shifts, gap emerges)
2. Author situation changes (gains new credentials, co-author partnership)
3. Differentiation becomes clear (new angle discovered)
4. Book market evolves (new audience emerges)

**Example:** "LabFlow Leadership" archived April 2026 because viability was 3.6/5. Could reopen in 12 months if:
- Author establishes speaking/consulting credentials
- Specific frameworks are defined and validated with 5+ PIs
- Distribution strategy is mapped and tested

## SUCCESS METRICS FOR THE SYSTEM

**How GHOSTWRITR measures if validation is working:**

### Quality Metrics
- % of books reaching outline with score ≥3.5: Target 100%
- % of books completing (finishing & published): Target 80%+ (vs. industry 40%)
- % of books achieving 4.0+ viability score: Target 70%+
- Average viability score of books that succeed in market: Target 4.2+

### Efficiency Metrics
- Average time in Promise Stage (validation): Target 6-8 weeks
- % of books requiring revision before proceeding: Target 40-50%
- Average time to re-validate after improvements: Target 2-4 weeks

### Market Metrics
- Average sales per book: Target 2,000+ copies (vs. industry 500)
- Book completion rate by readers: Target 60%+ (vs. industry 40%)
- Word-of-mouth/recommendation rate: Target 50%+ of readers
- Author satisfaction with market viability clarity: Target 90%+
