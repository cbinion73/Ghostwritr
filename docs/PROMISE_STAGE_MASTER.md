# 📖 GHOSTWRITR: Promise Stage Master Guide

## **Executive Overview**

The **Promise Stage** is the second critical phase in the GHOSTWRITR workflow. It transforms the foundational work from **Book Setup** (voice blend, targets, guardrails) into a **crystallized book promise**—the core commitment to the reader.

**Time to Complete:** 1-3 hours  
**Key Deliverable:** Validated, market-tested book promise statement  
**Success Gate:** Market Viability Score ≥ 3.5/5  
**Next Stage:** Outline (when committed)

**What Claude Does With Them:**
- Reads and indexes them

- Uses them for validation context
- Ensures promise aligns with source material

**User Experience:**
```
1. Click "Choose Files"
2. Select one or multiple files
3. Files appear in list (can remove individually)

```

### **Step 1B: Submit Initial Direction**

**Purpose:** Give Claude your book concept in your own words

**Prompt to User:**
> "Describe what your book is about, who it's for, and what you hope it will accomplish."

**Example Inputs:**
- "I want to write a practical guide for lab leaders transitioning into management roles, combining scientific rigor with modern team dynamics."
- "A book about decision-making frameworks for overwhelmed leaders."
- "How to build systems that scale without losing culture."

**What Claude Does:**
1. Analyzes the direction + uploaded files + voice blend
2. Generates initial **Promise Statement**
3. Populates all **6 tabs** with:
   - Audience description
   - Core truth/insight
   - Transformation arc
   - Market analysis
   - Positioning recommendations
4. Redirects to Promise editor with conversation started

## **📑 Phase 2: The Six Promise Tabs**

After wizard completes, you see the **Promise Editor** with 6 tabs. Each tab is editable.

### **Tab 1️⃣: Promise Statement** (Currently Viewing)

**What It Is:**
The **core promise to the reader**. What will they gain by reading this book?

**Characteristics:**
- 1-3 sentences
- Specific and concrete
- Compelling and clear
- Influenced by your voice blend

**Example:**
> "This book equips leaders with a proven framework to streamline complexity, enhance outcomes, and provide clear, actionable guidance that inspires followers."

**How Voice Blend Manifests:**
- Andy's clarity: "streamline complexity"
- Drucker's decisiveness: "proven framework"
- Jobs' inspiration: "inspires followers"

**User Actions:**
1. Read the generated promise
2. Edit if needed (direct textarea editing)
3. Click **Save Promise** when satisfied
4. System auto-validates on save

**Validation Check:**
- Persona Match: Does it sound like your voice blend?
- Specificity: Is it concrete, not vague?
- Market Alignment: Does it resonate with your audience?

---

### **Tab 2️⃣: Audience**

**What It Is:**
Crystal clear definition of **WHO** this book is for.

**Includes:**
- Primary audience (role, seniority, age range)
- Secondary audiences
- Psychographics (values, pain points)
- Current situation/problem
- What they want/need

**Example:**
> "Lab leaders (age 30-50) transitioning from individual contributor to management"
> - Currently: Overwhelmed, struggling to delegate
> - Pain point: Complexity management without losing scientific rigor
> - Wants: Clear frameworks, practical examples, permission to lead differently

**Why It Matters:**
- Validates that your promise reaches the right people
- Informs market sizing
- Guides Outline stage (chapters speak to this audience)
- Informs Draft stage (examples, tone, references)

**User Actions:**
1. Review generated audience
2. Edit to be more specific if needed
3. Add psychographics if missing
4. Save changes (auto-saves on tab switch)

---

### **Tab 3️⃣: Truth**

**What It Is:**
The **core insight or truth** that the book is built on. The fundamental belief being revealed.

**Characteristics:**
- Surprising or counter-intuitive (ideally)
- Foundational to everything in the book
- Universal but expressed through your voice
- The "why" behind the promise

**Example:**
> "Leadership effectiveness comes from clarity about complexity, not complexity avoidance. The best leaders don't simplify—they make the complex understandable and actionable."

**Why It Matters:**
- Gives the book **philosophical coherence**
- Guides chapter outlining (each chapter reveals this truth)
- Helps validate promise (is promise consistent with truth?)
- Differentiates from competitors

**User Actions:**
1. Review the truth statement
2. Ensure it feels right (your core belief)
3. Edit if too generic or not specific enough
4. Check: Does promise deliver on this truth?

---

### **Tab 4️⃣: Transformation**

**What It Is:**
The **before → after** journey for the reader.

**Structure:**
```
BEFORE (Current State)
- Feelings: Overwhelmed, uncertain, defensive
- Behaviors: Avoiding delegation, over-managing
- Outcomes: Burnout, team disengagement

AFTER (Transformed State)
- Feelings: Confident, clear, empowered
- Behaviors: Delegates effectively, systemic thinking
- Outcomes: Thriving team, sustainable pace

DURING (How the Book Bridges)
- Frameworks introduced (3-5 key systems)
- Examples provided (lab-specific stories)
- Practices offered (exercises, checklists)
```

**Why It Matters:**
- Shows the **value proposition** concretely
- Validates market appeal (do readers want this transformation?)
- Informs Draft stage (must show evidence of transformation)
- Guides chapter structure (build toward transformation)

**User Actions:**
1. Review current before/during/after
2. Make sure it resonates with your audience
3. Ensure each piece is specific and believable
4. Check: Is transformation real and achievable with book's content?

---

### **Tab 5️⃣: Market**

**What It Is:**
The **market viability analysis** for your book.

**Includes:**
- Total Addressable Market (TAM) sizing
- Competitive landscape
- Market trends
- Positioning vs. alternatives
- Commercial viability assessment

**Validation Scores Checked Here:**
- Market Size (is audience big enough?)
- Differentiation (how is this unique?)
- Demand (is there proven demand?)
- Timing (is market ready?)
- Monetization (can this succeed commercially?)

**Why It Matters:**
- **Gate:** Must score ≥ 3.5/5 to proceed
- Informs business case (is this worth writing?)
- Guides positioning (how to market it)
- Validates outline (are chapters addressing market needs?)

**User Actions:**
1. Review market analysis
2. Check market sizing numbers (feel right?)
3. Review competitive analysis
4. Ensure positioning is compelling
5. **This tab triggers gate validation**

---

### **Tab 6️⃣: Recommendations**

**What It Is:**
Specific **positioning and marketing recommendations** based on the promise and market.

**Includes:**
- Positioning statement (for marketing)
- Key differentiators
- Recommended formats (book + course? + newsletter?)
- Audience acquisition strategy
- Marketing angles

**Example:**
> "Position as the 'systems thinking guide for lab leaders' — unique in combining scientific methodology with leadership systems. Key differentiator: science-backed frameworks, not anecdotes."

**Why It Matters:**
- Informs the **Marketing Handoff** stage
- Guides author's messaging (how to talk about the book)
- Validates market assumptions
- Prepares for launch

**User Actions:**
1. Review recommendations
2. Highlight ones you agree with
3. Edit or add your own recommendations
4. Use these when creating marketing materials later

### **Button 2: Validate Promise**

**What It Does:**
Runs a **comprehensive validation** across 4 dimensions:

#### **Dimension 1: Persona Match** (Score: 0-5)
- Does promise align with voice blend?
- Does tone match Andy + Drucker + Jobs?
- Is writing quality consistent with personas?

**Sample Feedback:**
```
✓ Strong Andy influence: "clarity" and "actionable"
⚠ Could use more Drucker: "decision frameworks" too subtle
✓ Strong Jobs influence: "inspires followers"
```

#### **Dimension 2: Market Viability** (Score: 0-5) 🔴 **GATE**
Evaluates on 11 dimensions:
1. **Market Size** — Big enough audience?
2. **Differentiation** — How unique?
3. **Demand Proof** — Evidence readers want this?
4. **Competition** — Not oversaturated?
5. **Author Credibility** — Can author deliver?
6. **Trend Alignment** — Is market moving this way?
7. **Pricing Power** — Can this command premium?
8. **Audience Reach** — How accessible are readers?
9. **Monetization** — Multiple revenue streams?
10. **Evergreen Appeal** — Timeless or trendy?
11. **Publication Timing** — Market ready now?

**Scoring:**
- 4.5-5.0: Strong market opportunity
- 3.5-4.4: Viable with positioning (✓ Passes gate)
- 2.5-3.4: Risky, needs refinement
- <2.5: Not viable, rethink concept

**Gate Rule:**
```
If Market Viability < 3.5:
  → Cannot commit Promise
  → Must refine
  → Must revalidate
```

#### **Dimension 3: Promise Quality** (Score: 0-5)
- Clarity: Is promise clear and specific?
- Specificity: Concrete or vague?
- Compellingness: Would reader care?
- Alignment: Does it match audience + truth?
- Uniqueness: Does it differentiate?

**Sample Feedback:**
```
✓ Clear and specific
✓ Aligned with truth statement
⚠ Could be more compelling/surprising
✓ Differentiates from mainstream leadership books
```

#### **Dimension 4: Triangulation** (Check: OK/Issues)
Does the **triangle close**?
```
        PROMISE
        /      \
    AUDIENCE  MARKET
         \    /
        TRUTH
```

**Checks:**
- Does promise attract the intended audience?
- Does audience exist in the market?
- Does market demand the truth being taught?
- Does truth lead to the promised transformation?

**Sample Feedback:**
```
✓ Promise attracts lab leaders (audience)
✓ Lab leaders in the market (market)
✓ Market values clarity frameworks (truth demand)
⚠ Ensure transformation examples are lab-specific
```

**Process:**
1. Click **Validate Promise**
2. Button becomes disabled (showing "Validating...")
3. System runs validation (takes 10-20 seconds)
4. Shows **Validation Dashboard** with all scores
5. Highlights gaps and suggestions
6. If score < 3.5: Shows "Cannot Commit" until refined

---

### **Button 3: Refine with AI**

**What It Does:**
Uses Claude to **improve the promise** based on validation feedback.

**Process:**
1. Takes all gaps/suggestions from validation
2. Analyzes current promise
3. Rewrites using **your voice blend**
4. Presents refined version
5. You review before saving

**Example:**
```
ORIGINAL:
"This book provides frameworks for managing teams."

FEEDBACK:
- Too generic
- Missing voice blend
- Could be more specific

REFINED (Andy 50% + Drucker 30% + Jobs 20%):
"This book equips leaders with proven systems to transform 
complexity into clarity—making the difficult decisions fast, 
keeping teams aligned, and inspiring confidence at every level."
```

**User Actions:**
1. Click **Refine with AI**
2. Review the refined promise
3. Edit further if needed
4. Click **Save Promise** to apply

**Note:** Only works after validation (needs to know gaps)

---

### **Button 4: Save Promise**

**What It Does:**
1. Persists edited promise to database
2. Logs provenance event: "Saved promise statement"
3. Auto-triggers validation (if not recently validated)
4. Shows updated scores

**When You Should Click:**
- After editing promise directly
- After refinement AI suggests changes
- Before committing (validation will auto-run)

**Post-Save:**
- All tabs auto-save on switch, but Promise tab requires explicit save
- Validation auto-runs unless you've just validated
- Scores update if changed

## **💬 Phase 4: Conversational Refinement**

### **The "Refine" Panel** (Right Sidebar)

**What It Is:**
A **conversational chat interface** where you can discuss your promise with Claude.

**Purpose:**
- Discuss promise refinements conversationally
- Get clarity on suggestions
- Ask hypothetical questions
- Explore variations

**Example Conversation:**
```
USER: "Should the audience be broader? What if I target all leaders?"
CLAUDE (in your voice blend): "Lab leaders are your strength—your 
unique value is the science-to-leadership translation. Broader might 
dilute that. Better to be the clear choice for lab leaders than the 
okay choice for everyone."

USER: "OK, but should I mention scientific method specifically?"
CLAUDE: "Yes, but through the lens of 'evidence-based frameworks' 
rather than 'scientific rigor'—that feels more Andy/Drucker/Jobs."
```

**Features:**
- Messages persist in database
- Both user and assistant messages shown
- Responsive and conversational
- Uses your voice blend for tone
- Can be collapsed (→ button) to hide

**User Actions:**
1. Click on Refine panel (right side)
2. Type message or question
3. Press Enter to send
4. Claude responds in your blended voice
5. Continue conversation
6. Changes are NOT auto-applied (you decide what to use)


### **Reading Validation Scores**

**Persona Match (0-5)**
- 5.0 = Perfect voice blend expression
- 4.0+ = Strong blend presence
- 3.0 = Adequate but could be more
- <3.0 = Doesn't sound like your voice

**Action if Low:**
- Click "Refine with AI" to rewrite with blend
- Or manually edit to sound more like your blend

**Market Viability (0-5)** 🔴 **CRITICAL GATE**
- 5.0 = Excellent market opportunity
- 4.0+ = Strong market fit
- 3.5+ = ✓ Passes gate (can proceed)
- 3.4 = ✗ Below gate (cannot commit)
- <3.0 = Major market issues

**Action if Low:**
- Review specific dimension scores
- Identify weakest dimensions
- Consider: Pivot promise? Target different market? Add market evidence?
- Refine and revalidate

**Promise Quality (0-5)**
- 5.0 = Crystal clear, compelling, specific
- 4.0+ = Strong promise
- 3.0 = Adequate but generic
- <3.0 = Needs work (vague, weak)

**Action if Low:**
- Make promise more specific
- Add concrete language
- Use voice blend more explicitly
- Use "Refine with AI" for suggestions

**Triangulation (Check)**
- ✓ = Everything aligns
- ⚠️ = Some misalignment (see suggestions)
- ✗ = Major disconnect

**Action if Issues:**
- Review each corner of triangle
- Adjust promise/audience/truth alignment
- Use conversational refinement to explore

## **🔄 Commit Promise**

1. **Status Changes:** Stage status → COMMITTED

Yes—click "Recommit Promise" anytime to:
- Edit promise

## **⚠️ Common Issues & Solutions**

### **Issue: Validation Score Too Low (Market Viability < 3.5)**

**Causes:**
- Market too small
- Too much competition
- Unclear positioning
- Weak differentiation

**Solutions:**
1. Review the specific dimension breakdowns
2. Consider: Is the market actually smaller than expected?
3. Try: Reposition to a niche within the market
4. Consider: Add unique angle (combine markets?)
5. Refine promise to be more specific
6. Revalidate

**Example Fix:**
```
ORIGINAL (Too Broad):
"A leadership development book"

REPOSITIONED (More Viable):
"Leadership systems for lab managers 
transitioning from science to people leadership"
```

---

### **Issue: Promise Doesn't Sound Like My Voice Blend**

**Causes:**
- Generated template was generic
- Persona Match score low

**Solutions:**
1. Click **Refine with AI** (will rewrite with blend)
2. Or manually edit to sound more like you
3. Use sidebar chat to discuss your voice
4. Revalidate to check persona match

---

### **Issue: Validation Scoring Seems Wrong**

**Causes:**
- Algorithm misunderstanding market
- Feedback too generic
- Market assumptions unclear

**Solutions:**
1. Review detailed feedback in dashboard
2. Click "Refine with AI" to get suggestions
3. Use sidebar chat: "Why is market viability low?"
4. Provide more evidence (e.g., reference materials)
5. Recommit and revalidate

---

### **Issue: Can't Proceed to Outline**

**Causes:**
- Gate not passed (Market Viability < 3.5)
- Promise not committed

**Solutions:**
1. Check validation scores
2. Click "Validate Promise" if not recently run
3. Use "Refine with AI" to improve weak areas
4. Save refined promise
5. Revalidate to confirm gate passed
6. Click "Commit Promise" when ready

## **Appendix: Validation Dimensions Explained**

### **Market Viability: 11 Dimensions**

**1. Market Size**
- Is there a large enough addressable market?
- Scoring: >1M potential readers = high, 100k-1M = medium, <100k = low

**2. Differentiation**
- How unique is this book vs. competitors?
- Scoring: Novel approach = high, incremental = medium, me-too = low

**3. Demand Proof**
- Is there proven market demand?
- Scoring: Bestsellers in category = high, some evidence = medium, theoretical = low

**4. Competition**
- How saturated is the market?
- Scoring: <5 major competitors = high, 5-20 = medium, >20 = low

**5. Author Credibility**
- Can the author deliver on the promise?
- Scoring: Industry expert = high, relevant experience = medium, new voice = low

**6. Trend Alignment**
- Is the market moving toward this topic?
- Scoring: Hot trend = high, stable = medium, declining = low

**7. Pricing Power**
- Can this command premium pricing?
- Scoring: High-value promise = high, medium = medium, commodity = low

**8. Audience Reach**
- How accessible are target readers?
- Scoring: Easy to reach = high, medium effort = medium, hard to reach = low

**9. Monetization**
- Multiple revenue streams possible?
- Scoring: Book + course + consulting = high, book only = medium, niche = low

**10. Evergreen Appeal**
- Will this book remain relevant?
- Scoring: Timeless principles = high, framework stable = medium, trending topic = low

**11. Publication Timing**
- Is the market ready now?
- Scoring: Perfect timing = high, OK timing = medium, early/late = low

Each dimension contributes to overall Market Viability score.
