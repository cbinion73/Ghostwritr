# Personal Stories Integration Stage
## Author Story Sourcing and Integration - Operational

---

## PHASE OVERVIEW

After External Stories Integration stage is complete (external story collections locked), Personal Stories Integration stage captures the author's own stories, experiences, and case studies for chapter integration.

Author provides personal narratives—their own transformations, client successes they've directly witnessed, research they've conducted, lived experience examples. System integrates personal stories alongside external stories as proof and permission points.

---

## TRIGGER

External Stories Integration stage complete (all chapter external story collections locked).

Author begins Personal Stories stage by providing personal narratives and experiences per chapter.

---

## WORKFLOW

### Step 1: Author Selects Chapter and Provides Personal Story

Author navigates to chapter and provides personal story:
- Author's own transformation or learning journey
- Direct client success they've witnessed or guided
- Research study they conducted or participated in
- Personal experience that illustrates the chapter's big idea
- Professional case study from author's practice
- Interview with a client or research participant (author-conducted)

**Formats accepted:**
- Author-written narrative
- Interview transcript or notes (author conducted)
- Expanded notes or journal entries
- Structured reflection on client work
- Research summary from author's work
- Combination of multiple personal experiences

### Step 2: System Extracts Personal Story Elements

For each personal story, system identifies and extracts:

**1) Story Core**
- Author's or protagonist's starting point
- What the author discovered or transformed through
- Specific breakthrough or insight moment
- Current application or outcome
- Why the author is qualified to teach this
- What credential or experience gives this authority

**2) Author Authority**
- Author's relevant credentials/experience
- Why author is qualified to speak on this
- Length/depth of author's engagement with this topic
- Personal transformation or mastery level
- Connection to the chapter's framework or claim

**3) Narrative Elements**
- Personal vulnerability or authenticity signals
- Specific details that make the story vivid and credible
- Emotional honesty or transformation arc
- Practical outcomes or tangible results
- Metrics or data if author has them
- How this differs from external stories (author's unique insight)

**4) Usage Recommendations**
- Best placement in chapter (opening authenticity, middle proof, permission point)
- How to use in drafting (as credibility anchor, proof, lived example, vulnerable moment)
- What framework/claim it proves from author's practice
- Word count allocation for this story
- Connection to author's overall message/mission

**5) Tier Assessment**
- **Personal Tier A:** Fully developed, metrics/outcomes clear, author has deep experience
- **Personal Tier B:** Well-developed, outcomes clear, author has meaningful experience
- **Personal Tier C:** Emerging story, outcomes implied, author developing this work

Note: Personal stories are inherently credible (author is the source), but tier reflects depth and development.

### Step 3: System Maps Personal Story to Chapter

Based on chapter's big idea, research dossier, and external stories:

**Positioning Logic:**
- Where does this personal story fit alongside external stories in the chapter?
- Does it provide author credibility for the overall chapter claim?
- Does it prove a specific part of the framework through lived experience?
- Does it provide a permission point ("I struggled with this too")?
- Does it bridge from external examples to reader transformation?

**Strategic Placement:**
- Opening stories (establish author credibility early)
- Middle stories (prove claims through author's practice)
- Permission points (author's vulnerability or struggle)
- Proof of outcome (author's transformation or client results)
- Closing stories (author's vision or integrated understanding)

**Integration Plan:**
- How this personal story complements external stories in chapter
- Whether this is primary proof point or supporting detail
- Author voice/tone considerations (this should sound like author)
- Any credentials or authority signals to include
- Whether to name client/participant or anonymize

### Step 4: System Quality Checks

Before presenting story mapping, verify:
- [ ] Story is authentic and specific (not generic)
- [ ] Author's credentials/authority clear for this topic
- [ ] Personal story adds something external stories don't provide
- [ ] Emotional authenticity or vulnerability signals present
- [ ] Outcomes are concrete and demonstrable (or honestly acknowledged if still developing)
- [ ] Tier assessment is honest (A/B/C appropriate)
- [ ] Placement makes strategic sense in chapter
- [ ] Story demonstrates author's lived understanding, not just theory
- [ ] Privacy of any clients/participants properly considered
- [ ] Story tone matches intended author voice for chapter

If any fail: Flag issues before presenting to author.

### Step 5: Author Reviews Story Integration

Author can:

**1) Accept the story**
- Story positioned in chapter
- Confirms tier and placement
- Story saved to chapter collection

**2) Edit the story**
- Adjust narrative for tone or emphasis
- Change placement in chapter
- Adjust tier if author sees the story differently
- Add credentials or authority signals
- Modify vulnerability or authenticity level

**3) Request clarification**
- Ask system how this complements external stories
- Request alternative placements
- Ask what specific claim this proves
- Request suggestion for related personal story

**4) Reject or replace**
- If story doesn't fit chapter well
- Provide reason/context
- Request different angle or approach
- Mark for future use in different chapter

### Step 6: Collect Personal Stories Across Chapters

Repeat Steps 1-5 for all chapters until each chapter has:
- At least one author/personal story (credibility and lived experience)
- Optional: 2-3 personal stories if chapter has multiple proof points

**Target:** 1-2 personal stories per chapter, showing author's development and expertise

### Step 7: Build Author Authority Map

Once all personal stories collected:

System creates Author Authority Summary showing:
- Which chapters use which personal experiences
- Author's expertise/credential progression through book
- How personal stories build author's overall message
- Credibility signals across entire book
- Whether author's voice is consistent and authentic throughout

### Step 8: Lock Personal Stories for Chapter

Once author approves chapter personal story assignments:

Personal story collection locked with chapter ID.

Stored with chapter:
- Personal story (full text, credentials, context)
- Author authority signals
- Placement and usage notes
- Integration with external stories
- Author approvals/edits
- Tier assignment
- Version and date locked

## USER ACTIONS

**Per-chapter personal story workflow:**

1. **Select Chapter and Provide Story**
   - Author selects chapter ID
   - Provides personal story or experience

2. **System Extracts and Positions**
   - System identifies story elements
   - Assesses author authority
   - Maps to chapter and external stories
   - Assesses tier (A/B/C)

3. **Review & Edit**
   - Author reviews story positioning
   - Can adjust narrative, placement, or tone
   - Can request alternative integration

4. **Approve & Lock**
   - Author approves placement
   - Story locked with chapter ID
   - Ready for chapter draft

5. **Collect Across Book**
   - Repeat for all chapters
   - Build author authority map

6. **Complete Personal Stories Phase**
   - All chapters have author stories (minimum)
   - Author voice consistent across chapters
   - Status: Personal Stories Integration COMPLETE

---

## COMPLETION CRITERIA

Personal Stories Integration stage complete when:

- [ ] All chapters have at least one author/personal story
- [ ] Author authority/credentials clear in each chapter
- [ ] Author's voice consistent across personal stories
- [ ] Personal stories add unique insight beyond external sources
- [ ] Author's transformation/mastery shown through personal stories
- [ ] Client/participant privacy properly handled
- [ ] Personal stories complement external stories effectively
- [ ] Tier distribution reasonable (mostly A + B personal tiers)
- [ ] Author reviewed all personal story placements
- [ ] Author approved and locked all personal stories
- [ ] All chapter personal story status: LOCKED ✓

## AUTHOR VOICE AND AUTHORITY

Personal stories should:
- Sound like the author (voice consistent with overall book message)
- Demonstrate author's expertise through lived experience
- Show author's transformation or mastery in the topic
- Provide permission points where author is vulnerable
- Build reader's trust in author's guidance
- Connect author's personal journey to reader's potential journey
